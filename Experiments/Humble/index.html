Humble-Nishiyama


This is an implementation of the Humble-Nishiyama Randomness game, circa 2016.



The game is simple: the human player picks a combination of three card colors, such as black-black-red or red-black-red, by clicking the colored buttons. Then the computer picks a different trio.



The player then clicks the "deal" button until either the player's or the computer's pattern emerges. The winner gets a point. Clicking "clear" resets the cards but doesn't reset the overall score.



The cards are completely random and without replacement, and the likelihood of any of the eight possible three-color combinations appearing in a random card draw is equal.



...so why does the computer win far more than half the games?
